Making an inference is like being a detective. It means using clues from a story, passage, or situation, along with what you already know, to figure out something that is not directly stated. When you read, authors don't always tell you everything. Sometimes, they expect you to read between the lines and draw conclusions based on the information that is given.

Making inferences helps you understand stories and texts more deeply. It also improves your critical thinking skills because you're using evidence to make a thoughtful guess about something you don't know for sure.

Inference is a skill that can help you become a better reader, thinker, and problem solver.
